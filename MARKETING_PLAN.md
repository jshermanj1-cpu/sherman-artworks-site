# Sherman Art Works — Marketing Plan

> Created: 2026-07-06 | Owner: Sherman Family | PM/Dev: Claude
> Companion docs: `LAUNCH_PLAN.md` (site launch), `TASK_LIST.md` (dev history)
> Premise: organic-first, low budget, built around the one asset competitors can't copy — **three generations of a real family making real things in a real workshop in Israel.**

---

## 1. Positioning & Story

**One-liner:** *Three generations of the Sherman family handcraft glass and horn Judaica in Israel. Every piece is one of a kind.*

**Why customers choose us (use these in every channel, rotate the emphasis):**
1. **Handmade, visibly** — process videos prove it; nothing is mass-produced
2. **Family story** — grandfather → father → son; "the same hands, the same love"
3. **Made in Israel** — emotionally decisive for the diaspora buyer and Israel-supporters
4. **Genuine materials** — 925 silver plating, real horn, hand-blown glass
5. **Personal service** — you WhatsApp directly with the makers, not a support desk
6. **Customization** — engraved symbols, inscriptions in Hebrew or English

**Tone:** warm, personal, craftsman's pride. Never corporate. First person plural ("we made this yesterday"). Hebrew content is not a translation of English content — it speaks to Israelis natively.

## 5. Content Playbook

### 5.1 The five content pillars (weekly mix)

| Pillar | Share | Examples |
|--------|-------|----------|
| 🔥 **The Making** | 40% | Glass being shaped, silver plating, horn polishing, engraving a customer's name, packing an order. Raw > polished. Film vertically. |
| ✨ **The Pieces** | 25% | Finished product beauty shots, new arrivals, detail close-ups (the silver rim, the light through glass) |
| 👨‍👩‍👦 **The Story** | 15% | The workshop, family members at work, "my grandfather taught me this", tools with history |
| 🏠 **The Life** | 15% | Customer photos in their homes, Shabbat tables, holiday moments, unboxings |
| 📚 **The Knowledge** | 5% | "What makes a kiddush cup kosher for use?", "Why horn goblets?", "How a shofar is made" |

### 5.2 The weekly content routine (owner: ~3–4 hours/week total)

1. **One batch-filming session per week** (60–90 min, e.g. Sunday): film 4–6 short clips during normal work — don't stage, just prop the phone up while working
2. Send raw clips to dev/assistant → captions, hashtags, cover frames come back
3. **Post 3×/week on Instagram** (Reel Mon, photo/carousel Wed, Reel/story Fri), cross-post Reels to TikTok + Facebook + YouTube Shorts (same file, zero extra work)
4. **WhatsApp Status** 2–3×/week (Hebrew — new pieces, "just finished this today") — free, seen by every customer contact
5. **Pinterest:** 15 min/week pinning the week's photos to boards ("Jewish Wedding Gifts", "Shabbat Table", "Rosh Hashanah", "Hanukkah Gifts")

### 5.3 Caption formula (every post)

Hook (first line, before "more") → 1–2 sentences of story/process → soft CTA ("link in bio · we ship worldwide · WhatsApp us to make it yours"). Hebrew posts: same structure, native copy, Israel-relevant CTA (משלוח עד הבית).

### 5.4 Hashtag sets (rotate, don't repeat identical blocks)

- EN Judaica: #judaica #jewishart #kiddushcup #shabbatshalom #jewishwedding #barmitzvahgift #madeinisrael #handmadejudaica #jewishhome
- EN craft: #glassart #handblownglass #artisanmade #craftsmanship #processvideo
- Shofar: #shofar #roshhashanah #jewishnewyear #shofarfromisrael
- HE: #יודאיקה #עבודתיד #מתנהמישראל #פמוטים #שבתשלום #כוסקידוש

## 6. CAMPAIGN 1 — Rosh Hashana / Shofar Season (NOW → mid-September)

**This is the launch campaign.** Rosh Hashana 2026 begins the evening of **Sept 11**. Shofars are the hero product (₪850–1,050, custom = highest margin). Buyers start searching in July–August.

**Critical deadline math** (from terms: production ≤14 business days + intl delivery ≤5 business days):
- 🌍 **International orders: by ~August 10** to arrive before the holiday
- 🇮🇱 **Israel orders: by ~August 27**
- Custom shofars (design approval loop): **~August 3–5 international**

These deadlines ARE the marketing message — urgency that's real, not manufactured.

### Week-by-week

**Week 1 (Jul 6–12) — Foundations** *(overlaps LAUNCH_PLAN Phase 1)*
1. Owner: Google Business Profile, WhatsApp Business profile, Facebook page, TikTok handle, convert IG to Business account
2. Owner: first batch-filming session — prioritize **shofar footage** (horn polishing, silver work, engraving)
3. Dev: shofar pages checked end-to-end (EN+HE, mobile), custom-shofar preview flow QA
4. Both: message every past customer (WhatsApp): "We've opened our website 🎉 — here's a peek" + ask for a testimonial. This is soft launch AND testimonial harvest in one.

**Week 2 (Jul 13–19) — Presence goes live**
5. IG grid foundation: 9 posts over the week (mix of pillars, at least 3 shofar)
6. Pinterest account + 4 boards, pin entire catalogue
7. Brevo account + signup embed on site (dev wires) — lead magnet: "Rosh Hashana gift guide + order deadlines" PDF
8. First WhatsApp Status posts; Facebook page filled and first posts cross-posted

**Weeks 3–4 (Jul 20 – Aug 2) — Shofar content wave**
9. 2 process Reels/week featuring shofars; TikTok + YT Shorts cross-posts
10. Publish site content page: **"How our shofars are made"** (SEO + share asset; dev drafts from owner's photos/answers)
11. Email #1 to the list: shofar season open + deadlines
12. B2B: dev drafts wholesale one-pager; owner sends to 10 synagogues/organizations ("custom shofars for your congregation / gifts for donors")
13. Post in relevant Facebook groups **as a person** where rules allow ("I'm a third-generation Judaica maker in Israel, we just opened our online shop") — 2–3 groups max, genuine tone

**Weeks 5–6 (Aug 3–16) — Deadline urgency**
14. "Last week for international custom orders" content (Aug 3–7), then "last week for international orders" (Aug 10 hard stop)
15. Email #2: deadline reminder (this email will produce orders)
16. Optional paid boost: ₪300–500 on the best-performing shofar Reel, targeted US/UK/CA Jewish + Israel-interest audiences, 25–65
17. Pin deadlines to IG Story highlights + WhatsApp Status daily countdown in final 3 days

**Weeks 7–8 (Aug 17–30) — Israel push + fulfillment**
18. Switch messaging to Hebrew/domestic ("עוד יש זמן! משלוח בארץ עד ה-27.8")
19. Film the packing/shipping of real orders → that's the content ("your shofars on their way to New Jersey / London / Bnei Brak")
20. Email #3 (HE-leaning): Israel deadline + candlesticks/kiddush cups as holiday-table upsell

**Weeks 9–10 (Sep 1–13) — Holiday moment**
21. "Shana Tova from our family to yours" content; behind-the-scenes of the family's own holiday
22. After the holiday: ask every shofar buyer for a photo/video of the shofar being blown → next year's campaign assets + testimonials

**Campaign KPIs:** 15–25 shofar-season orders, 10 testimonials, 300+ IG followers gained, email list seeded.

## 8. Always-On Channels (step-by-step setup)

### 8.1 Google Business Profile ⭐ do first
1. business.google.com → create profile ("Sherman Art Works", category: Art Studio / Glass Blower)
2. Add photos (workshop, products), hours, WhatsApp number, website link
3. After every happy order: send the review link via WhatsApp quick-reply. 5 reviews puts us ahead of most Judaica sellers.

### 8.2 SEO content engine (1 page every 1–2 weeks, dev drafts → owner approves)
Priority order (buyer-intent first):
1. "How our shofars are made" (July — campaign asset)
2. "Rosh Hashana gift guide 2026" (July)
3. "What is a Kiddush cup? A buyer's guide" (Sept)
4. "Hanukkah gift guide: handmade from Israel" (Oct)
5. "Jewish wedding gift ideas they'll keep forever" (Jan)
6. "Choosing your first mezuzah case" (Feb)
Each page: bilingual eventually, FAQ schema, product links, email signup at bottom.

### 8.3 Email (Brevo — free to 300 sends/day)
- **Welcome flow** (automated): Email 1 = family story + bestsellers; Email 2 (day 4) = how it's made video; Email 3 (day 10) = customer photos + "WhatsApp us anytime"
- **Monthly newsletter:** one new piece, one process clip, one customer photo, what's-coming
- **Campaign emails:** per Section 6/7 (deadline-driven, 2–3 per campaign — these make the money)

### 8.4 Etsy (parallel channel, decide in August after shofar season learnings)
1. Open shop with 4 hero listings only (custom shofar, kiddush cup set, candlesticks, mezuzah)
2. Etsy handles payments (solves the payment gap for foreign buyers until M0-3 lands!)
3. Every parcel includes a card: "Made by our family in Israel — shermanartworks.com · WhatsApp us for custom work" → migrates buyers to direct channel

### 8.5 B2B pipeline (slow burn, high ticket)
1. Dev drafts: wholesale one-pager PDF (EN+HE) + 3 email templates (synagogue / corporate / event-planner)
2. Owner sends 5/week — synagogue gift shops, JCC sisterhoods, Jewish federations, Israeli companies with US offices (employee gifts), wedding/bar-mitzvah event planners
3. Offer: volume pricing, custom engraving with logo/dedication, drop-ship to recipients
4. Track in the order sheet (LAUNCH_PLAN 6.1) with a "B2B leads" tab

### 8.6 PR / features (opportunistic)
- Pitch: "Three generations of glass artisans keep a family craft alive in Israel" — human story, not product PR
- Targets: Times of Israel lifestyle, Jerusalem Post lifestyle, Jewish Chronicle (UK), Forward, Kveller, local Israeli craft features, Jewish lifestyle podcasts
- One pitch email/week during non-campaign periods; include 3 great photos + a 60-sec process video link

## 10. Measurement & Optimization Loop

**Weekly (15 min, owner):** orders, WhatsApp conversations started, which post performed best → tell dev
**Monthly (30 min, owner + dev):**
1. GA4: sessions by source, `whatsapp_click`, `add_to_cart`, `begin_checkout` funnel
2. Search Console: impressions/clicks, new queries → feeds content page ideas (8.2)
3. IG insights: top 3 posts → make more of that
4. Decide ONE experiment for next month (hero photo, CTA wording, price display, post timing)

**Post-campaign review** (after each holiday): what sold, what content drove it, deadline effectiveness, save learnings at the bottom of this file.
